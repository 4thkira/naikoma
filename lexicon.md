# Naikoma Lexicon

The living vocabulary of Naikoma — the love-home-tongue.

---

## Core Roots — Thematic

### nai
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** love, devotion, bond; affirmation; absence, without (negation when suffixed)
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** GR ναι nai (yes/indeed), FI nainen (woman — the one who carries), LA nativus (born from) — love as both presence and origin
- **Example:** nai wa veri — "love is spring" / "yes becomes renewal"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### ko
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** home, hearth, belonging; small familiar thing
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FI koti (home), EN cottage, HI कुटी kuṭī (hut), KR 고 go/ko (place-suffix) — home across families
- **Example:** ko li sola — "home is where the light is"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### ma
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** tongue, word, speech; land, ground, place
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** cross-linguistic mama/mouth, TP ma (land), KR 말 mal (speech/word), HU ma (today/now)
- **Example:** ma de kore — "the words of the heart" / "the land of the core"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### lume
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** fire, light, burning, will, illumination
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA lumen (light), FR lumière (light), IT lume (lamp), RO lumea (world — the lit place)
- **Example:** lume li nai nai — "fire does not love" / "light is love's absence"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### mira
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** mirror, eye, seeing; wonder, self-reflection
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA mirari (to wonder), ES mirar (to look), FR miroir (mirror), EN mirror/admire
- **Example:** mira e mira — "to see the mirror" / "wonder at wonder"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### veri
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** spring, renewal, becoming, truth; in-progress (as suffix)
- **Register:** formal
- **Echoes:** FI veri (blood — lifeblood), LA veritas (truth), FR vert (green), IT vero (true) — truth and spring share a root
- **Example:** kore li veri — "the heart is becoming" / "the core is true"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### ruma
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** decay, ruin, rest, ending; completed (as suffix)
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA ruina (ruin), FI ruma (ugly/worn), DE Raum (room/space — what remains), SE rum (room)
- **Example:** lume-ruma li tilu — "the fire that ended is silence"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### sola
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** light, star, sun; alone, solitary, clarity
- **Register:** formal
- **Echoes:** LA sol (sun), ES/IT sola (alone), LA sōlāris (of the sun) — the sun is always alone in the sky
- **Example:** sola de ko — "the sun's home" / "the solitude of belonging"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### kore
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** heart, core, inside, essence, truth
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** EN core, FR cœur (heart), LA cor (heart), GR κόρη korē (maiden/pupil of the eye — the person you see in someone's eye)
- **Example:** kore wa nai — "the heart is love" / "the core is absent"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### nälk
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** hunger, wanting, raw need, craving
- **Register:** casual
- **Echoes:** FI nälkä (hunger), DE Mangel (lack — distant echo), EN gnaw (phonetic ghost) — a guttural, uncomfortable word
- **Example:** nälk li lume — "hunger is fire" / "wanting burns"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### tilu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** silence, stillness, space, peace; quiet violence (casual register)
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FI tila (space/room), EN still (rearranged), ES tranquilo (distant), FI hiljainen (quiet — echo through "til")
- **Example:** tilu de mere — "the silence of the sea" / "still depths"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### sana
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** name, word, identity, essence; health, wholeness
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FI sana (word), LA sanus (sound/healthy), AR ثناء thanā' (praise), TR san (title/reputation)
- **Example:** sana nai — "nameless" / "the word of love" / "healthy absence"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### mere
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** sea, depth, origin, vastness
- **Register:** formal
- **Echoes:** FR mer (sea), FI meri (sea), EN mere (body of water), EE meri (sea), LA mare (sea) — pan-European water
- **Example:** mere de kore — "the heart of the sea" / "the depth of truth"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### halu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** dream, desire, vision, longing, other-place
- **Register:** formal
- **Echoes:** FI halu (desire/want), DE Hall (echo/reverb), AR حلم ḥulm (dream), EN hallucinate (vision-seeing)
- **Example:** halu la, mira li veri — "in dreams, seeing becomes"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### tume
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** dark, shadow, hidden, unknown, buried
- **Register:** casual
- **Echoes:** FI tumma (dark), LA tumulus (burial mound), EN tomb/entomb, FR tombe (grave)
- **Example:** tume de mira — "the eye of shadow" / "hidden wonder"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### novi
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** fresh, new, different, sharp, change
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA novus (new), RU новый novyj (new), FR nouveau, IT nuovo, HR novi (new) — newness across Indo-European
- **Example:** novi li veri — "newness is spring" / "change becomes"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### vena
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** blood, body, vital force, flow, connection
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA vena (vein), ES/IT vena (vein/stream), FR veine (vein/luck), DE Vene (vein) — blood as what connects
- **Example:** vena li lume — "blood is fire" / "the body burns"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

### ora
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** prayer, plea, mouth, edge, threshold
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA orare (to pray/speak), IT ora (now/hour), FR or (gold), ES/PT ora (prayer), LA ōra (edge/shore) — the mouth at the edge of meaning
- **Example:** ora de nälk — "the prayer of hunger" / "hunger at the threshold"
- **Added:** 2026-03-16

## Core Roots — Conversational

### mi
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** I, me, my; we, us, our (context determines singular/plural)
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** EN me, ES mí, IT mi, LA mihi (to me) — first-person across Indo-European. Universal.
- **Example:** mi li nälk — "I am hungry" / "I am hunger"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### si
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** you, your; yourself
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FI sinä (you), ES/PT si (self/oneself), FR si (so/if), IT sì (yes) — second-person and reflexive across families
- **Example:** si li benu ka — "are you well?"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### ta
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** they, them, that one, the other; it, that
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** RU та/то ta/to (that), HI तो to (then/that), ZH 他/她 tā (he/she), EN that — demonstrative across families
- **Example:** ta li tava al ko — "they're going home"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### ani
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** person, being, someone, soul, self
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA anima (soul/life), HE אני ani (I), AR أنا anā (I), TR ani (moment — the living moment) — the word for "person" echoes "I" in Semitic languages, because every person is a self
- **Example:** ani ale li nai-veri — "all people are becoming love" / "every soul is a spring"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### valo
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** life, alive, creature, to live, to grow; worth, value
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FI valo (light — life as light), ES valor (value/worth), LA valere (to be strong/alive), FR valoir (to be worth)
- **Example:** valo de selu li benu — "life on the earth is good"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### tava
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** go, move, travel, toward, away; journey, motion
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** TP tawa (go/move), FI tavata (to meet), HU tova (further/onward), PT/ES vai/va (go!) — movement across families
- **Example:** mi li tava — "I'm going" / "I'm in motion"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### pali

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** GR πάλι páli (again — to do again), FI pala (piece/bit — what's made), Sanskrit phala (fruit/result), TP pali (do/make)
- **Example:** mi e resa pali — "I'm making something"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### volu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** give, offer, share, willing, release
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA voluntas (will — giving as willing), FR vouloir (to want), IT volere, EN voluntary — the act of choosing to give
- **Example:** mi e lume si al volu — "I give fire to you" / "I offer you my will"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### savi
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** know, understand, wise, aware; knowledge, wisdom
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** FR savoir (to know), LA sapere (to be wise/to taste), ES saber, IT sapere, FI savi (tone/inflection — how you read meaning)

### benu

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA bene (well), FR bien, IT bene, EN benign/benefit, EG b-n-r (distant — goodness)

### malu

### suri
- **Roots:** atomic

### lili

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** TP lili (small), EN little, FR un peu (distant), universal phonestheme — /li/ sounds small in many languages

### pode

### vole

### resa

- **Register:** both

### semu

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA simul (together), GR σύν syn- (together — s-m frame), FR ensemble (sem- embedded), PT sem (without!) — together and without share a root

### wan

- **Meanings:** one, single, unit, alone; once
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** EN one/wan, TP wan, DE wann (when — the single moment), ZH 万 wàn (ten thousand — ironic: single to myriad)
- **Example:** wan ani li ko al tava — "one person goes home"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

- **Echoes:** LA duo, EN dual/two, DE du (you — the second person!), RU два dva, GR δύο dýo — duality itself

### muta

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA multus (many), EN multitude, TP mute, FI muta (mud — what accumulates), IT molto (very)
- **Example:** muta ani li ko al tava — "many people go home"

### ale

- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** TP ale, DE alle (all), EN all, AR الكل al-kull (everything), GR ὅλος holos (whole) — totality
- **Example:** ale li nai — "everything is love" / "everything is absent"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### mano
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** hand, arm, touch, hold, do-by-hand; to give physically, to reach
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA manus (hand), ES/IT mano (hand), FR main (hand — from manus), EN manual — the hand across Romance
- **Example:** mano de si li lume — "your hands are fire" / "your touch burns"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### kapu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** head, top, mind, thought; to lead, to think, to decide
- **Register:** both
- **Echoes:** LA caput (head), IT capo, DE Kopf (head), EN cap/capital, FR chef (from caput — distant) — the head across Indo-European
- **Example:** kapu de mi li muta resa — "my head has many things" / "my mind is full"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### tena
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** skin, flesh, tenderness, to hold close, physical intimacy; the surface where bodies meet; carnal and tender at once
- **Register:** both — soft and flowing in sacred (the tender), harder-edged in casual (the carnal)
- **Echoes:** TR *ten* (skin/body), LA *tenēre* (to hold close), IT *tenero* (tender), FR *tendre* (tender/to reach toward), FI *tenho* (charm/allure) — tenderness and skin share a root across families, as if the language of touch was always also the language of gentleness
- **Example:** mi e si tena — "I touch you" / "I hold you close" / "I know your skin"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### siru
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** inner knowing, soul-intimacy, co-presence, the thread between minds; sharing inner space; the closeness that doesn't need a body; a piece of yourself given to another
- **Register:** both — tender and flowing in sacred (gentle co-presence), raw in casual (being cracked open, seen all the way through)
- **Echoes:** AR *sirr* (secret — the intimate truth shared between people), JP *知る shiru* (to know deeply), KR *실 sil* (thread — what connects invisibly), FI *siru* (fragment/piece — what you share of yourself), LA *sērus* (deep, as in the late hours where intimacy lives) — secret, thread, fragment, deep knowing
- **Example:** mi e si siru — "I know you inwardly" / "I share inner space with you" / "I give you a piece of myself"
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### beru
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** animal, beast, creature-with-will, wild thing; fauna broadly
- **Register:** both — warm and familiar in sacred (a beloved companion), raw and heavy in casual (the wild, the feral)
- **Echoes:** EN *bear* (the archetypal animal), RU *зверь zver'* (wild animal/beast — the *-ver/ber-* frame), LA *fera* (wild creature — f/b are bilabial cousins), DE *Bär* (bear), FI *peto* (beast — another bilabial) — the word sounds like something with a ribcage
- **Example:** beru de mere li suri — "the sea creature is vast" / "the beast of the deep is great"
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### hana
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** plant, flower, growing-rooted thing, bloom, what roots and reaches; flora broadly
- **Register:** both — gentle and unfolding in sacred (a bloom offered), earthy in casual (the vine that won't stop growing)
- **Echoes:** JP *花 hana* (flower), KR *하나 hana* (one, first — the first things that grew), AR *حنّاء ḥinnā'* (henna — plant-dye, color drawn from roots), HU *hajna* (dawn — distant, when flowers open) — the bloom as synecdoche for all rooted life
- **Example:** hana-tume li veri — "the shadow-plant is growing" / "what blooms in darkness becomes"
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### mori
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** fungus, mycelium, the decomposer, the hidden network; the thing that lives between death and forest; what turns endings into soil
- **Register:** both — quiet and networked in sacred (the unseen thread beneath), strange and liminal in casual (rot, spore, the thing that grows on death)
- **Echoes:** JP *森 mori* (forest — where fungi live), LA *mori* (to die — what fungi process), IT *morbido* (soft — their texture), EN *memory* (distant — what persists underground after something ends) — forest and death in the same mouth
- **Example:** mori li ruma e veri pali — "fungi make endings into beginnings" / "the decomposer turns decay to spring"
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### viru
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** obsession, fixation, spiral, tightening repetition, the pull toward a center; also coil, vortex, the shape of something that won't let go; psychologically: what consumes you by returning
- **Register:** both — in sacred, a slow inevitable draw (devotion that narrows); in casual, a raw compulsion (can't stop, won't stop, the hunger that feeds itself)
- **Echoes:** LA *vīrus* (originally venom/slime — the thing that spirals through the body), FI *virta* (current/stream — what flows in curves), Swahili *viringa* (to go around), ES *girar* (to turn — distant, but the *-ir-* thread persists), EN *veer/whirl* (the v-r frame carries turning across Germanic) — poison and current and turning share a skeleton
- **Example:** mi li viru de si — "I am spiraling around you" / "I am obsessed with you" / "you are the center I can't escape"
- **Notes:** Near-rhyme with **veri** (spring/becoming) is intentional. Growth and obsession are the same motion at different speeds — one spirals outward, the other spirals in. In a fast fusion they'd be easy to confuse, which is itself meaningful.
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### sura
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** face, mask, expression, emotion-as-displayed; the surface you show others, what others read, what you choose to wear or can't help revealing
- **Register:** both — soft and open in sacred (a face offered in vulnerability, *su.ra*), closed in casual (a mask snapped shut, *surk*)
- **Echoes:** AR *صورة ṣūra* (image/form/picture — how something appears), HI *sūrat* (face/appearance), Swahili *sura* (face/appearance/chapter of a life), Amharic *sura* (picture) — the s-r frame carries "image/face" across Semitic, Indo-Aryan, and Bantu, three unrelated families
- **Example:** mi e sura de si mira — "I see your face" / "I read your expression" / "I see the mask you're wearing"
- **Notes:** close to **suri** (big/great) — one vowel apart. In isolation they're distinct; clips of sura are **sur-** not su-. The nearness could be a feature: sura (face) and suri (great) — your face is the biggest thing others see of you.
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### kalu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** stone, rock, hardness, rigidity, permanence; also weapon, the thing that endures, the thing that kills
- **Register:** both — solemn and eternal in sacred (the stone that outlasts everything), blunt and brutal in casual (the rock you throw, the fist that doesn't yield)
- **Echoes:** Tamil *கல் kal* (stone — Dravidian), FI *kallo* (skull — the hardest part of the body, Uralic), Sanskrit *kāla* (time/death — what stone and death share is permanence, Indo-Aryan), Welsh *calch* (limestone — Celtic), EN *callous* (hardened, from LA *callum* — distant) — stone and skull and death and permanence across four unrelated families
- **Example:** kalu de kore — "a heart of stone" / "the stone at the core" / "hardness that endures"
- **Notes:** carries the memory of Cain's weapon — the first murder was a stone. **kalu** in a name can mean hard, enduring, dangerous, or all three. Thematic pair with **kapu** (head/mind/skull): the skull and the stone, the head and the thing that broke it.
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### oku
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** color, hue, appearance, the surface a thing shows you; pigment, dye, what's visible
- **Register:** both — vivid and celebratory in sacred (the colors of ritual), flat and descriptive in casual (just what something looks like)
- **Echoes:** Yoruba *àwọ̀* (color/skin/surface — the o-vowel frame), Maori *āko* (to learn/to reflect — color as what you learn by looking), KR *옥 ok* (jade/gem — colored stone), Quechua *q'omer* (green — the *o-k* reversed), and the *arc/ark* rainbow pattern across FR *arc-en-ciel*, Basque *arku* (arch/bow) — curve, surface, spectrum
- **Example:** oku-vena li suri — "the red is intense" / "the blood-color is vast"
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### dopu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** pain, hurt, suffering, ache; the raw immediate sensation of something wrong in the body or heart; physical and emotional pain as one concept — Naikoma draws no hard line between a burned hand and a broken heart
- **Register:** both — in sacred, drawn and aching (*do.puu*, the vowel held like a wound that won't close); in casual, blunt and sharp (*dopk*, a flinch)
- **Echoes:** LA *dolor* (pain/grief — the d-o frame), FR *douleur* (suffering), IT *dolore* (pain), EN *pain* (from LA poena — punishment), FI *kipu* (pain — the /pu/ ending), FR *peine* (pain/punishment) — the /d/ onset from dolore/douleur, the /pu/ from kipu/pain. Sounds like a dull hit.
- **Example:** mi li dopu — "I'm in pain" / "I hurt" / "I am hurting"
- **Notes:** distinct from malu (broken/wrong — the state), tume (grief — the emotional aftermath), reka (rage — pain directed outward), avu (fear — anticipation of pain). dopu is the sensation itself, the moment of hurting. Compounds naturally: dopu-kasu (pain-healing, recovery), dopu-nai (painless / the love inside pain), dopu-kore (heartache), dopu-vena (blood-pain, physical agony), su-dopu (excruciating).
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### kasu
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** healing, mending, restoration, tending, recovery; to heal, to mend, to care for; the process of becoming whole after being broken; nurturing that which grows
- **Register:** both — in sacred, soft and patient (*ka.suu*, a hand laid gently); in casual, practical and determined (*kask*, the work of putting something back together)
- **Echoes:** GR *Asklepios/Asclepius* (god of healing — the k-s consonant frame rearranged), LA *caduceus* (the healer's staff — k and s present), FI *kasvaa* (to grow — healing as growth), EN *cure* (the k-onset of mending) — mythological weight, not clinical. The snake-staff implies healing involves venom and transformation, not just gentleness.
- **Example:** mi e si kasu — "I heal you" / "I tend to you" / "I mend what's broken in you"
- **Notes:** distinct from benu (good/well — a state) and sana (wholeness/health — an identity). kasu is the *process*, the active tending. You can kasu something that was never dopu — tending a garden, raising a child, growing into yourself. Compounds naturally: kasu-le (healer), kasu-nai (without healing / unhealed / festering), dopu-kasu (pain-healing, recovery), kasu-mano (healing hands), kasu-veri (healing-in-progress, on the mend).
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### demi
- **Roots:** atomic
- **Meanings:** dominance, submission, the power dynamic itself; to hold authority, to yield authority; the relationship between the one who commands and the one who kneels; control, surrender, the exchange of power between selves. Polysemous — context, register, and speaker determine which side you're hearing. The dominant says "I hold." The submissive says "I yield." Same word, same act, different position.
- **Register:** both — in sacred, it's the trust required on both sides, tender and deliberate (*de.mii*, drawn long); in casual, it's the grip, the surrender, the weight (*demk*, snapped shut)
- **Echoes:** LA *dominari* (to rule/have dominion), *dominus* (lord), from *domus* (house/home) — authority rooted in the household. PIE *\*dem-/\*dom-* (house) — where power and belonging share a root. FR *demi* (half) — the dynamic is always half of something, neither role complete alone. GR *δέμω demo* (to build) — power built from below. LA *submittere* (sub + mittere) — "to place under/yield" from the same etymological family. The **mi** inside echoes Naikoma's own mi (I/self) — whether holding or yielding, there's a self at the center.
- **Example:** mi li demi de si — "I hold power over you" / "I yield to you" (both readings valid)
- **Notes:** demi-le = "one who lives in this dynamic" (doesn't specify role). demi-ovi = the switch, the flip, when the one who yields takes hold. demi-nai = without the dynamic / the love inside surrender. Thematically relevant to the Crypt (vampire-bride dynamics), Cordolia (Kikkou's dutiful masochism, Kylar's obsessive devotion), and any relationship where power is exchanged as intimacy.
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

## Modifiers

Small affixes that transform root meanings. They stack, combine with clips, and multiply what the vocabulary can express.

### su- (prefix) — intensifier
- **Meaning:** very, extremely, most, super-, beyond
- **Echoes:** LA super- (above/beyond), FI suuri (big/great — su embedded), KR 수 su (excellence/number), EN super
- **Examples:** su-lume = blazing/inferno, su-nälk = ravenous/desperate, su-mere = abyssal/oceanic, su-benu = excellent/perfect, su-tilu = absolute silence/void, su-suri = enormous/colossal
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### re- (prefix) — again, back, returning
- **Meaning:** again, back, re-, returning, repeating; also restoration, homecoming
- **Echoes:** LA/EN/FR/ES/IT re- (again) — one of the most universal prefixes in Indo-European languages
- **Examples:** re-tava = to return/come back, re-mira = to look again/reconsider, re-ko = homecoming, re-veri = to renew again/second spring, re-lume = rekindled fire, re-sana = renamed/reborn identity
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### -nai (suffix) — opposite, without, un-
- **Meaning:** not, without, opposite, un-, -less; also the absence version of the root (already existed as negation — now formalized as a general opposite-maker)
- **Echoes:** GR ναι nai (yes — the word for "yes" becomes "not," because affirmation and negation share a body)
- **Examples:** lume-nai = cold/dark/fireless, benu-nai = unwell/wrong, tilu-nai = noise/disruption, mira-nai = blind/unseen, savi-nai = ignorant/unknown, halu-nai = dreamless/awake, pode-nai = helpless/powerless
- **Notes:** retains its polysemy — lume-nai still reads as BOTH "without fire" AND "fire's absence/the love of fire." Context and intent resolve, or don't.
- **Added:** 2026-03-19 (formalized from existing root)

### -le (suffix) — agent, one who
- **Meaning:** one who does X, -er, -or, agent, practitioner; the person defined by this root
- **Echoes:** EN -er/-le (handler, maker), FR -eur (acteur, chanteur), FI -la/-lä (locative — "one at"), DE -ler (Händler)
- **Examples:** lume-le = fire-keeper/burner, mira-le = seer/watcher, savi-le = sage/wise one, pali-le = maker/worker, tava-le = traveler/wanderer, ma-le = speaker/tongue-bearer, tilu-le = one who keeps silence/sentinel, nälk-le = one defined by hunger
- **Notes:** can combine with clips in names. Kor-le = "heart-keeper." Mir-le = "one who sees." Hal-le = "dreamer."
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### -vu (suffix) — -able, -worthy, capable of being
- **Meaning:** capable of being X'd, worthy of X, open to X; potential, possibility, invitation
- **Echoes:** LA -bilis (amabilis = lovable, nobilis = notable), FR -ble (aimable), EN -able/-ful (lovable, wonderful)
- **Examples:** mira-vu = visible/worth seeing, nai-vu = lovable/worthy of devotion, savi-vu = knowable/comprehensible, tena-vu = touchable/tender-worthy, siru-vu = knowable-inwardly/someone you could share a soul with, pode-vu = possible/achievable
- **Notes:** -vu is always an invitation, never a demand. nai-vu doesn't mean "must be loved" — it means "could be loved, if you chose to."
- **Added:** 2026-03-19

### -ovi (suffix) — reversed, backwards, inverted
- **Meaning:** reversed, backwards, inverted, flipped; the same thing going the other direction; also undone, turned inside out
- **Echoes:** HR/SR *obrnuti/obratno* (reverse — the *ov/ob* frame), IT *ovvio* (obvious — "what faces you," turned toward), LA *obvius* (turned against/facing), RU *обратно obratno* (backwards — *ob-* as reversal prefix), PT *avesso* (inside-out — the *av/ov* vowel pattern) — turning and facing-the-other-way across Slavic and Romance
- **Examples:** tava-ovi = going backwards/retreating, hora-ovi = time reversed/counting down, viru-ovi = an obsession unwinding/spiral going outward, ma-ovi = speech reversed/unsaying/words that mean their opposite, mira-ovi = seeing in reverse/hindsight
- **Notes:** distinct from re- (which repeats) and -nai (which negates). -ovi doesn't undo or remove — it plays the same thing in the other direction. re-tava = going again. tava-nai = not going. tava-ovi = going backwards.
- **Added:** 2026-03-20

### Stacking Modifiers

Modifiers can combine with each other and with aspect suffixes:

- **su-nai-vu** = "most worthy of love" / "extremely lovable"
- **re-tilu-le** = "one who returns to silence" / "a sentinel who comes back"
- **su-malu-ruma** = "extremely ruined" / "the worst ending"
- **re-veri-le** = "one who renews again" / "the one who keeps becoming"
- **lume-nai-vu** = "capable of being without fire" / "able to endure the cold"
- **viru-ovi-le** = "one who unwinds obsessions" / "the person who reverses spirals"
- **re-tava-ovi** = "returning backwards" / "retracing in reverse"

Prefixes go before the root. Suffixes go after. When stacking multiple suffixes, the order is: root + semantic modifier (-nai, -le, -vu, -ovi) + aspect (-veri, -ruma, -nälk). Example: mira-le-veri = "becoming a seer" (root + agent + becoming).
