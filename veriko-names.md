# VERIKO — Story World Names

**Status:** Work in Progress
**Language:** Naikoma (nai + ko + ma — "love-home-tongue")
**Setting:** A single world with distinct zones. Naikoma is an intimacy language — used for names, titles, and speech between loved ones, family, and faction. How much of a name you reveal, and how much the listener can parse, is itself an act of trust.

---

## Naming Structure

A Naikoma name has up to four positions:

```
[Role]  [Personal Name]  [Affiliation]  [Secret/Epithet]
```

- **Role** — What you are, stated with intention. Substituting the expected word (ani/person) with something else is a declaration.
- **Personal Name** — A deep fusion capturing who you are. The densest part. Outsiders hear a sound; insiders unfold a sentence.
- **Affiliation** — The group/family name, shared by all members. A compressed manifesto.
- **Secret** — Optional. Shared only with trusted people. Always spoken in sacred register, even if everything else is casual. The language insists.

Not every slot needs filling. How you read someone's name reveals how well you know them.

---

## Name Building Method

Vergil builds names; the analysis and documentation come after. The process has a consistent notation system:

### Step 1: List source words
Write out every Naikoma root or particle included in the name, separated by dots.

**Example:** `benu.hana.veri`

### Step 2: Mark surviving letters
Wrap the letters that survive into the final name in parentheses. Everything outside parentheses is dropped.

**Example:** `(ben)u.ha(na).ve(ri)` → **benari**

### Fusion Techniques

The technique choice is never arbitrary — it reflects the character's personality, source material, or thematic role:

- **Clip chain** — The standard. Take onsets or tails of each root and chain them. Most names use some version of this. *(Koamir, Okalberu, Okvedel, Limehana, Veriva, okmekalu, kadele)*
- **Overlap blend** — Two roots share a consonant or syllable at their boundary, compressing further. *(Timalu: ruma and malu overlap at ma; posdemi: pos and pode overlap at po)*
- **Tail-clipping** — Keep the end of each root instead of the beginning. Used deliberately for esoteric or poetic characters. *(Benari: ben from benU, na from haNa, ri from veRi — Yi Sang's esoteric poetry style)*
- **Aggressive onset extraction** — Strip roots down to their first consonant or consonant cluster. Used for maximum density. *(Spira: so + po + ira in two syllables)*
- **Particle-in-name** — A grammar particle (de, al, va, so) baked directly into a name slot. Makes the name read like a sentence fragment. *(Denälk: de + nälk; Tumal: tume + al; Velueva: le + va; sodemi-nai: so + demi + nai)*
- **Direct stack** — Monosyllabic roots placed side by side with no clipping. *(Misita: mi + si + ta; minone: mi + novi + ne)*
- **H-drop** — The /h/ in hana or halu drops when it falls mid-name, leaving the vowel to bridge. *(Benari: hana → a bridge between ben and ri)*
- **Deliberate clunkiness** — Intentionally awkward construction. The name sounds wrong because the character's relationship to selfhood is wrong. *(Okuhahana: says "plant" twice; Gonzo's entire name stutters)*
- **Register-shifting truncation** — Dropping a final vowel to close a syllable, pushing toward casual register. *(Vanail: -le → -l)*

### Design Threads

Some clusters have shared construction rules that carry thematic meaning:

- **The Crypt:** All three Sakamaki personal names contain **malu** (bad/wrong/broken). The wrongness is the family inheritance.
- **Spierce-adjacent names:** Tend toward two positions (no affiliation, no secret). The people closest to her don't armor their names.
- **va-names:** Three names bake the assertion particle *va* into their structure (Velueva, Veriva, and Tumal uses *al*). Each uses assertion differently — demand, declaration, direction.
- **okve- prefix:** Shared color-blood prefix across Kouha (okvedel) and Morgiana (okvedeberu). Same red, different reasons.

## The Crypt — viru-ovi

*Dialovers cluster. Bloodied Tongues.*

**Affiliation name: viru-ovi** — "obsession-reversed" / "the spiral unwinding." A structural mirror of 逆巻 (Sakamaki), which literally means "reverse spiral" / "winding backwards." The Japanese name and the Naikoma name say the same thing through completely different phonetic and semantic systems — the kind of cross-linguistic echo the whole language is built on.

**Shared secret: minone** — mi + novi + ne — "we're changing, right?" / "I'm becoming different, aren't I?" The *ne* makes it a question, not a statement — tentative, seeking agreement. For a group defined by cyclical damage, the secret name is a question about whether cycles can break.

**Design thread:** All three brothers carry **malu** (bad/wrong/broken) somewhere in their personal name. The wrongness is the family inheritance.
